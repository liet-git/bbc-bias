T-E-X-T-S-T-A-R-T. Palestinian media are reporting there were deadly Israeli air and artillery strikes overnight and on Thursday morning in the Zeitoun neighbourhood of Gaza City, in northern Gaza. The official Wafa news agency cited medical sources as saying that dozens of people were killed. A Wafa correspondent said that a number of homes and other buildings were targeted in the vicinity of the Ali bin Abi Talib mosque, 8 Street, the Hassan al-Banna mosque and the University of Gaza. Many of the buildings were set on fire, they added. The Israeli military said it had launched an operation in Zeitoun and that it carried out strikes on about 25 “terror targets” in Zeitoun. Wafa and Hamas-affiliated news outlets also said at least five people were reportedly killed in a strike on a home belonging to the Eid family in the west of Rafah, in the south of Gaza. They included three children, one of them a one-year-old infant, according to the Kuwaiti hospital. A photo taken by Reuters news agency also showed a group of men praying next to seven body bags. At least two of which appeared to be marked with the surname “Eid”. Smoke rises from Gaza City's Zeitoun neighbourhood on Thursday morning. Palestinians pray beside the bodies of people reportedly killed in Israeli strikes in Rafah. T-E-X-T-E-N-D.
