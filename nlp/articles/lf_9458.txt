The leader of Hamas has said the group are "committed" to their demands for a ceasefire in Gaza - including asking for the withdrawal of the Israeli military from the territory. "We are committed to our demands: the permanent ceasefire, comprehensive and complete withdrawal of the enemy out of the Gaza Strip, the return of all displaced people to their homes, allowing all aid needed for our people in Gaza, rebuilding the Strip, lifting the blockade and achieving an honourable prisoner exchange deal," Ismail Haniyeh said in a televised speech. Earlier today, the prime minister of Qatar - a mediator in ceasefire talks between Israel and Hamas - said the main point of dispute was over the return of displaced people to different parts of the Palestinian territory.
