 F-N-S-T-A-R-T. 9006 F-N-E-N-D.  D-A-T-E-S-T-A-R-T. 2023-12-28 D-A-T-E-E-N-D.  T-I-T-L-E-S-T-A-R-T. Israel sentences first conscientious objector since Gaza offensive began. T-I-T-L-E-E-N-D.  T-E-X-T-S-T-A-R-T. An 18-year-old Israeli man has been tried and sentenced to 30 days in a military jail for refusing to enlist in the army, Israeli media reports. Tal Mitnick, who lives in Tel Aviv, entered an army recruitment centre to announce his refusal to enlist. He cited the Israel-Hamas war and occupation for his conscientious objection - when a person refuses to take part in something because of their beliefs. Mitnick is the first Israeli conscientious objector to be imprisoned since the start of the Gaza offensive. He could face repeated detention if he continues to object to military service.
