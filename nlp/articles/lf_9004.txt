 F-N-S-T-A-R-T. 9004 F-N-E-N-D.  D-A-T-E-S-T-A-R-T. 2023-12-28 D-A-T-E-E-N-D.  T-I-T-L-E-S-T-A-R-T. UN report documents human rights violations in West Bank. T-I-T-L-E-E-N-D.  T-E-X-T-S-T-A-R-T. The UN has urged Israel to end what it calls the “unlawful killings” of Palestinians in the West Bank. The UN’s human rights chief, Volker Turk, said he was extremely troubled by the country’s use of military tactics, disproportionate force and restrictions on movement. The report, released today, also accuses Israel of: Mass arbitrary arrests, detentions and reported torture and other ill-treatment by Israeli forces, raising concerns of collective punishment. Exponential increase in attacks by armed settlers leading to mass displacement of Palestinian herding communities. Ongoing discriminatory movement restrictions affecting daily life and choking the local economy. Speaking on the release of the report, Turk said the findings were “extremely troubling”, and while the violations of human rights were not new in the conflict, "the intensity of the violence and repression is something that has not been seen in years. ”. There was no immediate comment from Israeli officials on the report. Israel has said its operations in the West Bank are pre-emptive and aimed at curbing security threats. T-E-X-T-E-N-D.
