Moving away from the Middle East to bring you some news from the governing body of cricket in Australia which has told one of its top batters not to show an on-field message in support of Palestinians at an upcoming Test match with Pakistan. Usman Khawaja had planned to wear shoes bearing the words "all lives are equal" and "freedom is a human right". Australia's cricket authority has said Khawaja must abide by international rules prohibiting "personal messages". Captain Pat Cummins and Australia's sport minister have supported Khawaja, but he will no longer wear the shoes. You can read more here about the Usman Khawaja story.
