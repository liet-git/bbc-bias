Dr Khaled Saleh is the president and chief medical officer at the charity FAJR which aims to advance orthopaedic medical care in underserved countries. Speaking to the BBC from the European Hospital in Rafah, southern Gaza, he says the situation across the Strip is desperate. "About 80% of the hospitals - if not more - and in general the infrastructure to support medical care, have been decimated," he says. "And as a consequence of that we're seeing a number of significant casualties, significant delayed care, lack of supplies have been an issue, and combined with the current famine, decreased supplies available to replenish their immune systems are causing chronic and medical issues.
