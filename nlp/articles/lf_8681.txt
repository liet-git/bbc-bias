The Palestinian Red Crescent Society says five people have been killed and three injured in Israeli bombing on its headquarters in Khan Younis, a city in the south of the Gaza Strip. The humanitarian organisation said Israel's forces had been targeting the eighth floor of its headquarters. It also said drones had fired in the vicinity of the neighbouring Al-Amal hospital. It added that 14,000 displaced people were sheltering across the two sites. The BBC has approached Israel Defense Forces for comment. T-E-X-T-E-N-D.
