The UN General Assembly will meet on Tuesday to discuss the situation in Gaza, its president said. This comes after the US on Friday vetoed a UN Security Council resolution for a ceasefire - this was backed by almost all other members. The UK abstained. AFP news agency, which said it has seen a draft of the text, said it calls for "an immediate humanitarian cease-fire" as well as the "immediate and unconditional release of all hostages".
