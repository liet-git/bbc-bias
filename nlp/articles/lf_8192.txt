The fallout from the Speaker's unusual decision to allow Labour's amendment to the Gaza debate is continuing. Now the Clerk of the House, Tom Goldsmith, who's the chief adviser on matters of parliamentary procedure in the Commons, has added his views. He's written a letter putting on record his belief Lindsay Hoyle's decision was a "substantial breach" of procedures intended to allow the opposition to vote on their own motion. ". . . I feel compelled to point out that long-established conventions are not being followed in this case," he writes, adding he and Hoyle discussed the issue "extensively" and the final decision "is one for you to make".
